Group: Throughout the project, our use of Microsoft Teams can be seen as a positive. It allowed us to collaborate easily and all work on the one file simultaneously with many different editing tools at our disposal. Without the use of such a system, the team would not have been able to collaborate as well and perhaps would not have met any deadlines at all. One of the biggest drawbacks in this assignment was communication issues towards the end of the assignment and the project. In the beginning, the team had cohesive and thought-out plans on what we were going to do and who was going to do who. Delegations of tasks were set, and everyone did them as expected. However, when a group member caught an illness and was behind 2-3 days of information on the group report, communication between members suddenly were put on a halt and barely anyone spoke to each other, teams or private messaging. Communication is necessary in group projects, especially when someone is behind due to unforeseen circumstances and could have improved within our group. A new discovery that gave our group a mild surprise was that python had its own module just dedicated for chess. This allowed the team to have a starting foundation to work on. Another surprising discovery during the working on the project is how insurmountable the project seemed given the timeframe of the project, as well as the lack of resources freely accessible. It was an ambitious project idea, only hampered by the limited understanding of full extensive coding of a machine, given how even professional freelancers and hired employees spend at least several months minimum creating a serviceable engine, let alone a high-functioning competitive one. One of the most important things we have learned about groups is that huge commitments are needed to be taken when taking on a project this huge. This was evident as by the end of the assignment, there was still so much work to have been done and the project had much more potential than in its current state. But because due to personal circumstances some group members faced, we were unable to live up to that standard of the project reaching up to that level of potential. This was a lesson worth learning, and worth taking note of.
